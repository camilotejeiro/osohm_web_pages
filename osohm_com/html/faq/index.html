Frequently Asked Questions

Please note, these answers reflect only our views and are not in reference to any specific organization or social movement in particular.

  1. What is open technology?
    2. In broad terms, "open" means you have access to the underlying intellectual property that makes technology and its applications possible.
    • From a software standpoint: open software means you have access to the human-readable source code which is used to build the executable(binary) copies of the programs/applications you use.
    • From a hardware standpoint: open hardware means you have access to the necessary design files (electrical, mechanical, industrial ...etc ) which are used to mass manufacture multiple copies of the products you use everyday.
    • From a research standpoint: open research means you have unrestricted access to unbiased scientific knowledge. The knowledge that makes advances in technology possible, and the results that individuals, communities and governments can rely on to make better, more informed decisions.
  2. What is closed/proprietary Technology?
    3. Closed and proprietary means you don't have access to the intellectual property used for developing the technology and applications you use. You are granted limited permission to use the copies you purchase.
    • From a software standpoint: proprietary software means you don't have access to human-readable source code, it is used to generate the executable(binary) copies which you can then purchase(license) and use for a certain time under the applicable legal condittions (see EULA, Terms of Service).
    • From a hardware standpoint: proprietary hardware means you have don't have access to product design files. These are used to manufacture identical copies of consumer goods which you can then purchase, these products are normally shipped with terms and condittions which grant you certain rights to use the product but forbid you from opening, or reverse engineering the underlying hardware.
    • From a research standpoint: proprietary research means that relevant scientific information resides in the hands of a few.

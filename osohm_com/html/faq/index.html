Frequently Asked Questions

Please note, these answers reflect only our views and are not in reference to any specific organization or social movement in particular.

  1. What is open technology?
    2. In broad terms, open technology means you have access to the underlying intellectual property which makes the technology you use possible.
    • From a software standpoint: open software means you have access to the source code which is used to build the distributable copies of the programs/applications you use.
    • From a hardware standpoint: open hardware means you have access to the electrical and mechanical design files which are used to mass manufacture multiple copies of the products you use everyday.
    • From a research standpoint: open research means you have access to unbiased scientific knowledge. The knowledge that makes technology possible and the results that individuals, communities and governments can rely on to make more informed decisions.
